Posted on July 20th, 2016

A lot has happened in life over the past few days: Nicole and I celebrated our first anniversary in New York, Pokemon GO fad is still raging on, I've started picking up Bootstrap and RoR, I'll be working as a CS230 TA next semester, my bike was stolen from my apartment over the weekend, and work is almost coming to a close. And as important as these matters are, they can be written about some other time. Today I want to discuss something on a more serious note - current issues, especially the shootings/bombings.

So much has happened over the past month, nay, the past week, that I fear I might even grow a little oblivious to all the horrors taking place in this world. The main storyline here in the US has been the Black Lives Matter campaign following two separate casualties in Baton Rouge and Minnesota, which then (perhaps indirectly, but nevertheless) led to the police shootings in Dallas and Baton Rouge. As chaos unfolded in the States, France suffered from a horrific incident in Nice involving a deranged truck, Turkey faced a coup-de-etat, and people are being executed in Syria as I write these feeble words, feeling absolutely helpless.

When I was young(well, I guess I'm still young at 23, but you know what I mean), I wanted to be a diplomat, not just because I was more fluent in English than my Korean peers, but because I really did think that world peace would be attainable. Change happened somwhere along the way and now I call myself a computer engineer, but deep inside, until very recently a little kid inside myself still used to believe that world peace was indeed a thing that could be achieved if enough people really wanted it.

My bike was stolen while I was away for the weekend, and the moment I realized that I would need to spend $300+ on a new bike for commute, the first thought that came to mind was: "Wow, there sure are a lot of disgusting, horrible people out there." I believe in a good world, I really do. I profess to be one of the more naive minds amongst my peers, almost to the point of being childishly innocent. But situations like this make me realize that the world isn't honey and nectar after all, and that reality isn't as golden as the Peter Pan inside myself wants it to be. Yes, I know that a lot of it has to do with the fact that my parents have made so many sacrifices as to make me think that this world is indeed my little oyster. But I myself had also actively blocked out a lot of the negativity and instead only focused on the bright side of the world, possibly in hopes of tricking myself into thinking that doing so would somehow make this world a better place.

It didn't. No matter what I see and how I think, the cruel reality is that there are illogical terrorist attacks and unfortunate, heart-wrenching casualties taking place every single day (if you think it's an exaggeration, just look at this Wikipedia link of the list of terrorst attacks in July 2016 - the list is a lot longer than it should, and it sickens me.) A younger version of myself would have racked his head, looking to make sense of what obviously doesn't, and trying to find a solution that would rectify all wrongs. But now, all I can do is sit back while browsing through the thousands of articles delivering one bad news after another, feeling hopeless but unable to do anything to not feel so lost.

An ideal college essay would have my version of the answer right at about this point in the essay. But truth is that I don't have one, and I'm not sure if there would ever be an answer to this question - the question that mankind has not been able to solve since the dawn of time. I want to reassure my younger self and tell him that the world is as beautiful as he thought it was, but recent news makes me doubt whether I, or anyone else, for that matter, would be qualified to say anything of the sort.

And so I close my eyes yet another time with a heavy heart, hoping that tomorrow would be a tad better than today.

Posted on July 11th, 2016

I didn't think that I'd be writing this much about the NBA. But then again, I didn't think that the offseason would be this dynamic, with massive changes taking place every other day, or so it seems.

Big story of the day: Tim Duncan has retired. Now, I've never professed myself to be a TD fan - in fact, I had even tried to become his fan when I was in middle school, only to be disappointed by his lackluster highlight films. And even now, after 10+ years, I have yet to understand just how proficient he had been in the art of basketball.

And yet his retirement really stings because of what he had brought to the table for the past 19 years: humble greatness. Despite all the limelight that his fellow peers receive, not once has Timmy craved for attention - rather, he just stuck to what he did best, which was to win basketball games. In an era where exposure seems to be more valued than skills (Exhibit One: Nick Young, and the media exposure that he gets), Timmy really showed what it is like to be a humble superstar, an oxymoron that's much harder to live out than it is to say. That he had carried himself the way he did all throughout his career, despite being one of the all-time greats, should receive more accolades than any other NBA news has for the past few weeks, if not years.

The word respect has not been this suitable for a single individual in a long time. Much respect, TD.

Posted on July 4th, 2016

I woke up to a random Groupme text that a diehard OKC friend of mine had sent to another friend, a native of SF. The text read: "I hate your stupid basketball team!!!!!" It took a while for my still-asleep brain to process what had happened: that Kevin Durant announced his decision to join the Warriors.

This was disheartening on so many levels. One, he would no longer be joining the Celtics, my fav (I actually asked Nicole to get me a Durant Celtics jersey for our anniversary gift - I really did believe there was a chance). Two, another franchise player had left his team, making it one less superstar to follow the paths of the Stocktons, Kobes, Duncans, and Dirks (this was why the Derrick Rose trade to the Knicks made total sense on a basketball level, but not so much on an emotional level). And three, perhaps most importantly, the league will now be seeing another SuperTeam, this time in the form of the Splash Fam.

Charles Barkley famously noted back in 2012 that a superteam would never have been possible in the 80s and the 90s because players' egos were too big to join forces with their rivals. And heartbreaking as it may be that greats like him, Stockton, and Malone never got to win a single ring, it was probably the very aspect of the game that made it so much more interesting - that you really didn't have a strong favorite, and even the dynasty, the Bulls, always had opponents thwarting them at every moment.

Now, of course the Warriors will have their share of adversities. But everybody is going to take their championship status for granted, if they ever get there, and anything short of it would be considered a pathetic waste of talent. I've been a huge Steph Curry fan because of how humbly he carries himself and how electrifying his game is. The fact that LeBron was part of a SuperTeam himself back in Miami contributed a lot to why I rooted for GSW in the past two Finals. Now, Curry, Durant, and Thompson, three of my favorite players in the game right now, have joined forces. And unfortunately, it seems like basketball as we knew has taken a turn for the worse.

Posted on July 3rd, 2016

So, for the last three weeks I've been creating the anniversary project website for Nicole. The idea first hit me out of nowhere - quite literally, in that I was biking home after work, and the idea just struck me. I guess a lot of it had to do with the fact that I was just picking up basic HTML/CSS and JavaScript tutorials from Khan Academy. While racking my brains for what to get her, especially since she didn't want flowers(my go-to present up until now), I came up with an idea: why don't I create something from scratch? That way, it'll be a truly one-of-a-kind gift, one that I'd poured my heart into.

As I soon discovered, things were easier said than done. I had a whole idea sketched on paper, but implementing the nitty details via HTML/CSS wasn't the easiest thing in the world, at least partially because I had just started teaching myself the details. Minute implementations often took hours of Googling plus trial and error, and honestly, if it weren't for the fact that I had to make this work, I would probably have met myself halfway between and compromised with what I could make.

What started out as a small idea developed into a full-scale idea that would take weeks to complete. Not only did I have to create the skeletal structure for the website, I would also fill each page with a lengthy letter, complete with pictures. I've never been happier with iPhotos, which not only copies every photo from my iPhone to my MacBook, but also enables easy file transfers so that I wouldn't have to use a cloud server or anything. Best of all, going through all the pictures of us throughout the past year really made me realize just how much Nicole means to me.

The webpage has been hosted as a Github Project Page, with an external domain purchased through Google domain. Everything was a first for me, and it took some errors to get everything correct, but now things are good to go.

The link is as follows: raymeetsnicole.com. I plan on continuously updating the website with more of our adventures, so hold on tight - it's going to be a bumpy ride!
